Kachnar

Kachnar, scientifically known as Bauhinia variegata, is a flowering tree belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is native to various parts of South Asia, including India, Nepal, and Pakistan. Here are some key facts about the Kachnar tree:

Kachnar is a medium-sized deciduous tree that can grow up to 12-15 meters in height. It has distinctive bi-lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a camel's hoof, giving the genus Bauhinia its common name "Camel's Foot Tree."

One of the most striking features of the kachnar tree is its beautiful flowers. The tree produces large, showy, and fragrant blossoms in various colors, including shades of pink, purple, and white. These flowers are often used in culinary and decorative applications.

In some regions, the flowers of the Kachnar tree are used in cooking. They can be added to salads, curries, and other dishes. The flowers have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
