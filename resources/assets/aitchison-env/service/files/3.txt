Pipal
Pipal tree (Ficus religiosa) or Sacred Fig is a species of fig native to the Indian subcontinent and Indochina that belongs to Moraceae, the fig or mulberry family. It is also known as the bodhi tree. It is a large dry season-deciduous or semi-evergreen tree up to 30 metres (98 ft) tall and with a trunk diameter of up to 3 metres (9.8 ft).
It suitably grows at altitudes ranging from 10 meters (33 ft) up to 1,520 metres (4,990 ft). It is native to South East Asian countries. Pipal trees are tolerant to various climate zones and soils.
