Neem

Neem, scientifically known as Azadirachta indica, is a versatile and widely recognized tree that is native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the family Meliaceae. Here are some key facts about the neem tree:

Neem is an evergreen tree that can grow up to 15-20 meters (50-65 feet) in height. It has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and the tree is characterized by its dense and spreading canopy.

Neem is native to the Indian subcontinent but has been introduced and cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions around the world. It thrives in warm and arid climates.

The neem tree is famous for its medicinal properties. Various parts of the tree, including the leaves, bark, seeds, and oil extracted from the seeds, have been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Neem is believed to have antibacterial, antiviral, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used to treat a wide range of health conditions, from skin issues to digestive problems.
