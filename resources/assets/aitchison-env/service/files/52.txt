ASHOKA TREE

The Ashoka is prized for its beautiful foliage and fragrant flowers. It is a handsome, small, erect evergreen tree, with deep green leaves growing in dense clusters. Its flowering season is around February to April. The ashoka flowers come in heavy, lush bunches.

Compounds in the leaves and flowers of the Ashoka tree may reduce inflammation. Bioactive compounds in the Ashoka tree may reduce ulcers. Ashoka tree leaves and flowers may help to remove kidney stones. Parts of the  Ashoka tree may aid in expelling worms from the body. 

It may also act against cancer and can be used for bacterial infections, arthritis, ulcers and depression. Ashoka trees may protect the heart and help to reduce blood sugar levels.
