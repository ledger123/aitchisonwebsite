Anar

The Pomegranate (Punica granatum) is a fruit-bearing deciduous shrub in the family Lythraceae, subfamily Punicoideae, that grows between 5 and 10 m (16 and 33 ft) tall.

Pomegranate seeds are used as a spice known as anar dana.Pomegranate juice has long been a popular drink in Europe and the Middle East, and is now widely distributed in the United States and Canada.

Pomegranate trees can self-pollinate or be pollinated by insects. Pomegranates are high in phenols, tannins, folate, potassium and vitamin C and K. Each pomegranate contains hundreds of edible seeds which can be eaten fresh or made into juice.
