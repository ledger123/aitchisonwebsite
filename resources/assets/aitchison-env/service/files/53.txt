Ulta Ashoka 

Ulta Ashoka (Saraca Asoca) is a rainforest tree belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is an important tree in the cultural traditions of the Indian subcontinent and is given the status of ‘Holy’ by the Hindus and Jains. 

The flower of Ashoka tree is the state flower of the Indian state of Odisha. As a wild tree, the Ashoka is a vulnerable species. It is becoming rarer in its natural habitat, but isolated wild Ashoka trees are still to be found in the foothills of the central and eastern Himalayas, in scattered locations of the northern plains of India as well as on the west coast of the subcontinent near Mumbai.
