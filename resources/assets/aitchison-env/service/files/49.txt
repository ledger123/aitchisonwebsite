Poplar

Poplar trees belong to the genus Populus and are known for their fast growth and distinctive foliage. 

Poplar trees are deciduous and typically grow to medium to large sizes, depending on the species. They have simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. The leaves are often triangular or diamond-shaped and are usually green in color.

Poplar trees are found in various regions across the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, and Asia. They thrive in temperate climates with abundant sunlight and moisture.

Poplar trees are known for their rapid growth, making them a popular choice for timber production and paper manufacturing. Some species can grow several feet per year.
