Tun

"Indian mahogany" typically refers to a tree known as Toona ciliata, which is also commonly called "Indian Redwood" or "Meliaceae." It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is native to various parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asian countries. 

Indian mahogany or Tun is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters (approximately 100 feet). It has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and the leaflets are typically lance-shaped. The tree produces small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers.

The heartwood of Indian mahogany is highly valued for its quality and is often referred to as "Indian mahogany wood." It is reddish-brown to deep red in color, with a fine and straight grain. This wood is sought after for high-quality furniture, cabinetry, and woodworking.
