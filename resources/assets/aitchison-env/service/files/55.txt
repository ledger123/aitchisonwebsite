Gaub

Diospyros malabarica, the Gaub tree, Malabar ebony, black-and-white ebony, or pale moon ebony, is a species of flowering tree in the family Ebenaceae that is native to the Indian Subcontinent and South East Asia.

It is used externally to heal sores and wounds. When ripe, the fruit is beneficial in treating diarrhea and dysentery; blood diseases; gonorrhea, and leprosy. The fruit is also said to break a fever, to be an antidote for snake poisoning, and to be demulcent.

It is a small to medium-sized tree, up to 15-35 m tall. Its trunk can attain up to 70 cm in diameter. Its bark is dark or blackish in color. Its alternate, stalked leaves are oblanceolate, 7-32 cm long, emerging pink in color, and gradually turning beige then to dark green eventually.
