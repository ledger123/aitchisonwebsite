Silk-Cotton Tree

The Silk-Cotton Tree, scientifically known as Ceiba pentandra, is a large tropical tree native to various parts of the world, including Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It belongs to the Malvaceae family. 

The Silk-Cotton Tree is a massive deciduous tree that can reach towering heights of up to 70 meters (230 feet) or more. It has a straight trunk and a broad crown of large, palmate leaves.

The tree produces large, showy, and often fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white to pale pink and have five petals. They are pollinated by a variety of insects, including bees.
